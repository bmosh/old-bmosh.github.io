My Experience:

Space Exploration Technologies Corp.

I’m excited to be joining the Application Software team at SpaceX as a Software Engineering Intern this summer! As part of this internship, I'll have an opportunity to create mission critical applications that are used throughout SpaceX to accelerate launch vehicle production and flight, as well as systems that allow Starlink to grow into a worldwide fast, reliable Internet service.

MIT Lincoln Laboratory

As an AI/ML Engineering Intern at the MIT Lincoln Laboratory in the Summer of 2023, I led algorithm implementation and evaluation on a project that searched to ensure outcome fairness for legally protected groups in large-scale national security decision processes. This opportunity arose when the previous lead left to pursue a congressional fellowship, and I recognized my skills in PyTorch, Scikit-Learn, Pandas, and other python libraries were strong enough to add to the project’s codebase. After stepping up, my work allowed our team to effectively test algorithmic debiasing performance in a variety of ways, and report our findings to our sponsor on our original timeline.

OR Advances through Collaboration (ORACL)

I was recruited to join the OR Advances through Collaboration research program at Cornell by my Engineering Statistics and Probability II professor in September 2022. My first assignment was to the GM Team, as a co-lead for a team of 14 leading correspondence with the GM Advanced Analytics Center. Our team would work with them to model the potential impact of a shift from the dealership sales model, to more closely approximate the simplified direct sale model that companies like Tesla have been implementing in recent years. After one semester, I was moved to lead a team of my own, working with the Mars Wrigley company on their advertising models and retail demand forecasting.

Richardson Lab of Environmental Engineering

I joined the Richardson Lab of Civil and Environmental Engineering in February 2022, and was originally tasked with developing ImageJ scripts to automate the time-intensive process of algae classification and toxin level prediction. While working on this assignment, I realized that there was potential to automate the entire process, from the moment our volunteers submit data to when the predictions are returned to them. I earned a research grant from the university to continue my work over summer 2022, and during that time I implemented this full automation using Python, PowerShell, and the Google Drive and Mail APIs. Now, I am working to shift our system away from the Google Ecosystem with a redesigned standalone website, submission form and SQL Server to increase volunteer accessibility and trust in the program.

Cornell ORIE Undergraduate Leadership Society

I volunteer my time with the Cornell ORIE Undergraduate Leadership Society, designing and piloting a mentorship program that pairs upperclassmen ORIE majors with underclassmen to help with academics, internships, and more. On the ULS, I also work to plan, schedule, and carry out events and research expos such as the ORIE Fall Kickoff Event.

Phi Sigma Kappa

I serve as the current President of Phi Sigma Kappa’s Gamma Chapter, leading a diverse body of 75 men and a house operating on a budget of nearly $250k per semester. As president, I oversee all house activities, including recruitment and philanthropy. I am also our representative to the Interfraternity Council (IFC) and Office of Sorority and Fraternity Life (SFL), managing relationships and communication between these organizations and my chapter. I’m proud to say that my chapter maintained the highest average GPA of all IFC chapters last semester! Before taking over as President, I was elected as house manager, where I managed the maintenance and upkeep of the house. In this role, I planned and led a 3-day work party to sand, repair and paint the exterior and porch of the house, replace broken lighting, successfully preparing the house for annual city inspections at the start of the FA22 semester.

Cornell Engineers for a Sustainable World

I was accepted to the Engineers for a Sustainable World project team on the Biofuels/Bioenergy Implementation subteam during the fall of my freshman year. I quickly rose to subteam lead, reorganizing our project base and managing team recruitment. My team worked with the Barstow Lab of Biological and Environmental Engineering to research and provide economic analysis on alternative rare earth extraction processes under a DARPA grant. We also worked on a project to convert waste fats, oils, and greases from campus dining halls and Greek life houses into usable biodiesel for campus tractors in the summer months. I was a part of this team until the end of sophomore fall, allowing my successor to take over as my academic priorities had changed from chemical engineering to CS+ORIE by then.
